# Better Printing UK — Phone Call Spec

**Who to call:** Better Printing UK, **023 8087 8037** (sales/quotes line)
**Also:** info@betterprinting.co.uk
**Why them:** UK's only print supplier we've found that publishes perforated pads + raffle-tickets + general VDP as a single combined capability. They position VDP as a "please call" service on top of their standard pad products. Everyone else in the UK online market either does bulk-identical only (Solopress, Blue Chip, Quick Print UK) or POD premium pricing (Prodigi, theprintspace).

**Product to reference on the call:** **https://www.betterprinting.co.uk/note-cards/** — their Note Cards product. Their spec list for this product includes: A6, A5, DL, custom sizes; **Silk paper 250-400gsm** (exactly Amanda's spec); uncoated, premium uncoated, recycled, kraft paper also available; 4-colour CMYK. Website shows "£1.73 each" — which is almost certainly single-copy / no-VDP pricing. The call is specifically to get the real volume + VDP price.

## Context to give them up front (30 seconds)

> "We're Remi, a dog-show entry management platform in the UK. We're looking to handle the show-day printing for the clubs we work with — prize cards, ring numbers, judges' books and award boards — via a single supplier who can turn artwork around quickly and ship direct to club secretaries. Each club's cards are unique to their show (club name, show date, class name, placement colour) so we need true variable-data printing, not bulk-identical. We're expecting to grow to 50+ shows per year in the next 12 months."

## Prize card spec (the critical one)

- **Size:** A5 (148×210mm) or A6 (105×148mm) — we're flexible if A6 cuts cost
- **Stock:** 300-350gsm card, **silk finish** (not gloss — Amanda's tested gloss and hates the bleed; not matte — tested and it washes out)
- **Print:** full colour, single-sided
- **VDP requirement:** EVERY card has unique text AND unique coloured border/placement word. Six placement colours (1st red, 2nd blue, 3rd yellow, 4th green, 5th purple, 6th orange). Plus club name, show name, date, class name, placement text — all variable per card.
- **Form factor:** single cards, stacked loose. **Not** folded, **not** pre-bound into pads unless pads tear cleanly.
- **Artwork submission:** we can send a multi-page PDF (one page per card) OR a template + CSV, whichever they prefer.

**Target per-card price:** **≤23p delivered** for the spec above. We'll pay for quality + automation but there's a ceiling beyond which clubs won't pay.

## Ring number spec

- **Size:** A7 (74×105mm) — this is the UK dog-show standard
- **Stock:** card (250gsm+), probably plain matte or uncoated
- **Print:** B&W, single-sided, one big number
- **Numbering:** sequential per show (1-N)
- **Target:** **≤5p each delivered**

## Judges' books

- A4 or A5 saddle-stitched booklets
- ~20-30 pages each
- Matched pair (2 identical per show)
- Cost less critical — this is a one-off per show

## Awards board

- A2 or A3 single-sided full-colour poster
- 1 per show
- Cost not critical

## Critical questions to ask them (in priority order)

1. **Can they do TRUE variable data — different text AND different graphics (placement colour) on every card, not just a sequential number?** This is the deal-breaker. If they're raffle-ticket-only with fixed layout + variable number, we can't use them for prize cards.

2. **What's their price per prize card at these specs?** Push for both:
   - 75-88 cards per single show (just-in-time)
   - 1000 cards pooled across 10-15 shows in a single monthly batch

3. **What's their VDP upcharge?** Is it a per-card fee, a per-order fee, or baked into the unit price?

4. **What paper weights and finishes do they offer for the card product?** Specifically: do they have silk-finish 300-350gsm? (Their website only lists 120gsm uncoated as default — this is the biggest open question.)

5. **Do they dropship direct to third-party UK addresses?** (Club secretaries' homes, not ours.) What's the shipping cost to a single UK address?

6. **Artwork submission workflow:** do they accept a multi-page PDF (each page = one unique card) or do they require CSV + template model?

7. **What's the turnaround time** from artwork received to despatched?

8. **Minimum order quantity** — is there a floor below which they won't quote?

9. **Can they do A7 ring numbers at sequential numbering** at commodity rates? (We've seen Quick Print UK quote 1000 for £28 = 2.8p each — does Better Printing beat that?)

10. **Bundle discount** — if we commit to prize cards + ring numbers + judges' books + awards board together as a regular order, is there a bundle price?

---

## Red flags (walk-away triggers)

- Price above ~40p per prize card at single-show volumes
- VDP limited to numbers/codes (no text/image variation)
- Minimum order above 500 per show
- No dropship to third-party
- Turnaround > 10 working days

## Green flags (this is our supplier)

- Price at 20p or under per prize card with proper card stock + silk
- True text + graphic VDP
- Dropship included
- Turnaround 3-5 working days
- Willing to discuss a standing monthly order

---

## If they can't do it

Ask them: **"Who would you recommend for VDP on 300gsm silk card at our volumes?"** Trade printers know each other's specialities. Better Printing's a medium-size family firm — they'll likely know the right name even if it's not them.

---

## After the call — what to do with the answer

**If they quote ≤ 25p/card with full VDP + dropship:** we build. That's Remi's print-shop product for prize cards. Mixam stays for catalogues. Everything else (ring numbers, judges' books, awards board) rolls under the same Better Printing relationship if pricing works.

**If they quote 26-40p/card:** we hold as a premium-tier option alongside the Amanda-printed route. Clubs that want automated-and-premium pay more; clubs that want cheap-and-local stay on Amanda's Brother laser workflow.

**If they quote 40p+ or say they can't do the VDP spec:** that's the definitive "market gap" confirmation. We commit to the Amanda-on-Brother-laser plan for prize cards, raffle-ticket printer (Quick Print UK at 2.8p) for ring numbers, Mixam for catalogues + awards boards.
